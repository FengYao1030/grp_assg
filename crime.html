Cybercrime Threat Attack

Understanding and preventing digital threats in our connected world

The Digital Threat Landscape

Cybercrime is the fastest-growing criminal activity worldwide, with attacks becoming more sophisticated each year. Every 39 seconds, a new attack occurs, and 43% of these target small businesses.

Did you know? 95% of cybersecurity breaches are caused by human error, making awareness your first line of defense.

Common Attack Methods

Phishing: Deceptive emails or messages designed to steal sensitive information by impersonating legitimate organizations.
Malware: Malicious software including viruses, worms, and trojans that infect devices to steal data or cause damage.
Ransomware: Software that encrypts your files and demands payment for their release, often targeting businesses.

Essential Protection Strategies

Use strong, unique passwords for each account

Enable two-factor authentication everywhere

Secure your home network with encryption

Keep all software updated regularly

Cybercrime Attack Timeline: What to Expect

Reconnaissance (Days/Weeks Before)

Hackers research targets, scan for vulnerabilities, and gather intelligence

Initial Compromise (Seconds to Hours)

Attackers exploit weaknesses (phishing, malware, or unpatched systems) to gain access

Burglary Crime

Protecting your home and property from unauthorized entry and theft

Understanding Burglary Risks

Burglaries occur approximately every 1 Hours in the Malaysia, with 34% of burglars entering through the front door. Most burglaries happen between Night when homes family are all sleeping or not at home.

Key Fact: Homes without security systems are 300% more likely to be burglarized.

Common Burglary Methods

Unlocked Entry Points: 30% of burglars enter through unlocked doors or windows.
Forced Entry: Using tools to pry open doors, break windows, or force locks.
Surveillance: Burglars often watch homes to learn routines and identify vulnerabilities.

Home Protection Strategies

Install deadbolt locks on all exterior doors

Use motion-activated lighting around property

Install visible security cameras

Get a monitored alarm system

Burglary Timeline: What to Expect

Target Selection (Days/Weeks Before)

Burglars often scout neighborhoods looking for vulnerable properties

Entry (Under 1 Minute)

Most burglars spend less than 60 seconds breaking in

Theft (8-12 Minutes)

Quickly grab valuables like cash, jewelry, electronics

Financial Fraud Crime

Safeguarding your assets from deceptive financial schemes

The Fraud Landscape

Financial fraud costs consumers over $50 billion annually. 1 in 4 adults will experience some form of financial fraud in their lifetime, with seniors being particularly vulnerable.

Alarming Trend: Credit card fraud accounts for 45% of all identity theft reports.

Common Fraud Types

Credit Card Fraud: Unauthorized use of credit/debit card information for purchases.
Investment Scams: Fake opportunities promising high returns with little risk.
Identity Theft: Using someone's personal information to open accounts or make purchases.

Protection Measures

Monitor account statements regularly

Use credit monitoring services

Shred financial documents before disposal

Be skeptical of "too good to be true" offers

Fraud Warning Signs

  • Requests for upfront payment or gift cards
  • Unsolicited calls from "government agencies"
  • Unexpected checks with requests to send money back
Tip: Protect yourself from financial fraud by monitoring accounts regularly and never sharing sensitive details via phone/email.

Vehicle Theft Crime

Protecting your vehicles from theft and unauthorized use

Vehicle Theft Statistics

A vehicle is stolen every 2 Days in the Malaysia, with over 20,000 vehicles stolen annually. 65% of thefts occur because vehicles were left unlocked.

Hotspot Alert: Parking lots and residential streets account for 70% of vehicle thefts.

Common Theft Methods

Unlocked Vehicles: Thieves often check door handles for unlocked cars first.
Key Fob Relay: Using devices to amplify key fob signals to unlock and start vehicles.
Towing: Simply towing away vehicles from parking spots.

Protection Strategies

Always lock doors and close windows

Use steering wheel locks or brake pedal locks

Install GPS tracking devices

Park in well-lit, high-traffic areas

Identity Theft Crime

Safeguarding your personal information from misuse

Identity Theft Overview

Identity theft affects over 20 000 Malaysian annually, with losses exceeding $5 billion. It takes an average of 200 hours to resolve identity theft issues.

Warning: Social media oversharing contributes to 30% of identity theft cases.

Common Identity Theft Types

Financial Identity Theft: Using personal information for financial gain.
Medical Identity Theft: Using someone's identity to obtain medical care.
Tax Identity Theft: Filing fraudulent tax returns using stolen SSNs.

Protection Measures

Freeze your credit with all three bureaus

Secure important documents in a safe

Be cautious about sharing personal info

Monitor credit reports regularly

Recovery Steps if Victimized

Immediate Action

Place fraud alerts with credit bureaus and report to FTC

Within 1 Week

File police report and contact affected financial institutions

Ongoing

Monitor accounts and consider identity theft protection service

Phone Scams Crime

Recognizing and avoiding fraudulent phone calls and messages

Phone Scam Statistics

Malaysian lose over $20 billion annually to phone scams. 60% of scam calls impersonate government agencies, while 30% pretend to be tech support.

Current Trend: "One-ring" scams that call and hang up increased by 250% last year.

Common Phone Scams

IRS Impersonation Tech Support Lottery/Sweepstakes Grandparent Scam Bank Fraud Alert Debt Collection

Scam Call Red Flags

  • Caller demands immediate payment
  • Requests for gift cards or wire transfers
  • Asks for remote computer access
  • Claims to be from a government agency with threats

Protection Strategies

Register with Do Not Call Registry

Verify caller identity independently

Use call screening apps

Never share personal information

Tip: Avoid phone scams by never sharing personal/financial details with unknown callers. Verify caller identity, and hang up on suspicious requests for money or information.
